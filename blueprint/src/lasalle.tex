In this section, we prove La Salle's theorem on stability for autonomous systems.

We consider the system
\begin{align*}
  \dot{x}(t) = f(x(t))
\end{align*}

Let $K \subset X$ be compact.
We assume that the system has a fundamental solution $\Phi$ on $K$.

Limit set
\begin{align*}
    \Gamma^+(y) \coloneqq \{ p \colon \forall \eps > 0, \forall T > 0, \exists t > T, \norm{\Phi(t,y) - p} < \eps\}
\end{align*}

We set
\begin{align*}
    L \coloneqq \bigcup_{y \in K} \Gamma^+(y)\,.
\end{align*}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:lasalle1}
    The set $L$ is invariant under the flow.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
    We prove the stronger statement that $\Gamma^+(y)$ is invariant.
    Let $y \in K$ and $x \in \Gamma^+(y)$, we have to show that $\Phi(t,x) \in \Gamma^+(y)$.
    
    Let $\eps > 0$ and $T > 0$ be arbitrary.
    By definition of $\Gamma^+(y)$, it follows that for every $\eps > 0$ and $T > 0$ there exists $t > T$ with $\norm{\Phi(t,y) - x} < \eps$.
\end{proof}
